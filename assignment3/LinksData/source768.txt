<h1>The "Prix des Innovateurs"</h1>


<div class="intro-text">
Students, young graduates and young entrepreneurs now have until 29 February 2016 to enter the &ldquo;Prix des Innovateurs&rdquo; competition
</div>


Launched in December 2015, in spring 2016 the <strong>Innovators Award</strong> will reward project owners in the fields of <strong>Big Data</strong> and <strong>Energy Storage</strong>.&nbsp;<br />
<br />
A judging panel of eight personalities will select the best projects according to various criteria: the inventiveness of the project, maturity, feasibility, potential extensions and the quality of presentation.<br />
<br />
<strong>Students, graduates and young entrepreneurs now have <span style="text-decoration: underline;">until 29 February 2016</span> to submit their entries on the &ldquo;Prix des Innovateurs&rdquo; website:</strong> <a href="http://prixdesinnovateurs.arcelormittal.com/deposer-votre-candidature/" target="_blank">http://prixdesinnovateurs.arcelormittal.com/deposer-votre-candidature/</a><br />
<br />
Qualifying projects can be entered at two different stages of development: <strong>Ideas to be developed</strong>, which will subsequently require incubation, design and laboratory research or <strong>advanced design</strong>, the following stages being support, production engineering, prototyping, market testing and funding.<br />
<br />
In each of the two categories, the winners will receive a grant of 50,000 euros for the first, 10,000 euros for the second and 2,500 euros for the third in order to help them to bring their project to fruition. They will also be able to benefit from the support of ArcelorMittal experts.&nbsp;<br />
<br />
The judging panel comprises personalities from the business world, local authorities, higher education, the media, and representatives of ArcelorMittal:<br />
<strong><br />
Herv&eacute; Bourrier</strong><br />
<hr />
Herv&eacute; Bourrier is a graduate of the Bordeaux Ecole Nationale Sup&eacute;rieure de Chimie et de Physique and also has an MBA from Cedep/Insead. He is now CEO of ArcelorMittal France, President of the UIR (Union of Recycling Industries) and Vice-President of EPE (Enterprises for the Environment).<br />
<br />
<strong><br />
Carl de Mar&eacute;</strong><br />
<hr />
A civil engineer specialising in electromechanical engineering, Carl de Mar&eacute; joined the Group in 1988. He is now Vice President of ArcelorMittal, Head of innovation and technological development.<br />
<br />
<strong><br />
Gilles Le Borgne</strong><br />
<hr />
A graduate of the Ecole Nationale Sup&eacute;rieure des C&eacute;ramiques Industrielles, Gilles Le Borgne is now Director of Research and Development at PSA Peugeot Citro&euml;n group. He is also jointly responsible for the 2l/100 project within the New Industrial France programme, and Chairman of the Automotive Technical Committee within the Automotive Industry Platform.<br />
<br />
<br />
<strong>Fran&ccedil;ois Mudry</strong><br />
<hr />
A graduate of the Ecole Polytechnique and author of a thesis on "Modelling fracture in nuclear pressure vessel steels" Fran&ccedil;ois Mudry has notably been the scientific adviser in respect of ArcelorMittal R&amp;D projects. He is currently President of the Lorraine Technological Research Institute and Adviser to the High Commissioner of the French Atomic Energy Commission.<br />
<br />
<br />
<strong>Eric Niedziela</strong><br />
<hr />
An engineering graduate from ENSAM Aix en Provence, Eric Niedziela began his career at the Fos plant. He joined Business Division North in 2009 as Chief Technology Officer, and is now CEO of ArcelorMittal Atlantique &amp; Lorraine.<br />
<br />
<br />
<strong>Dani&egrave;le Quantin</strong><br />
<hr />
An engineering graduate from Ecole Centrale Paris, Dani&egrave;le Quantin has been a member of ArcelorMittal&rsquo;s research and development steering committee since 2007. She is now Global R&amp;D Director of Human Resources and coordination of research centres in France and Spain.<br />
<br />
<br />
<strong>Lucie Viry</strong><br />
<hr />
With a PhD in the physical chemistry of condensed matter and functional polymers, Lucie Viry was particularly interested in the study and development of innovative solutions applied to robotics and the medical sector. She is now responsible for R&amp;D in respect of materials solutions for a new additive manufacturing technology (3D printing) for Pollen AM.&nbsp;<br />
<br />
<br />
<strong>Patrick Weiten</strong><br />
<hr />
A municipal engineering graduate, Patrick Weiten was recently elected to be the first Vice-President of the Acal (Alsace Champagne-Ardenne Lorraine) Regional Council. He was President of the Moselle Department Council from 2011 to 2015, Mayor of Yutz from 1995 to 2011, and President of the Portes de France Thionville integrated local authority from 2008 to 2015.
