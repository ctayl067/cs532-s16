<h2 class='date-header'><span>Monday, 18 January 2016</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://bobmcnair.blogspot.co.uk/2016/01/my-move-to-france.html'>My Move to France</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-9039661334422964233' itemprop='description articleBody'>



 
 
 
 <style type="text/css">p { margin-bottom: 0.25cm; line-height: 120%; }</style>


<br />
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
I have not been
posting as regularly as I would have liked, I have had a lot going on
recently.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Things will be
changing for me very soon, I will be re-locating to France where I
have bought a small home. I live in rented accommodation here, and
there is no way with our dysfunctional property market I could afford
a home in the UK.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
I'm retiring from my
profession of the last 40 plus years, I will have to earn some extra
income in France, I cant afford complete retirement till I get my
State Pension.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
I do hope to be able
to spend more time on my Blog, and will be doing Video Blogging as
well.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br />
</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
All of course with a
Left wing pro Corbyn message, I will be in another Country but I will
keep up the fight against the Tories, and of course the Blairites who
have done as much damage to the UK and are trying to destroy our
Party.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br />
</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
I will cover more of
the reasons for my move another time, but it was essentially to give
me some sort of security in my older years. I had none in the Private
Rental Sector, and no other options open to me. It is a damning
indictment on our &#8220;Property Market&#8221;, both rental and owner
occupier, that the cost of the home I purchased in France, was equal
to just over two and a half years rent in my current home.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
I think you call
that sort of decision &#8220;a no brainer&#8221;. It was paid for with a
pension release. I am happy its a wise move, my pension savings now
benefit my retirement, rather than funding someone else&#8217;s.</div>

<div class='post-outer'>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://bobmcnair.blogspot.co.uk/2016/01/p-margin-bottom-0.html'>The current "Crisis" and the troublemakers.</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-2389639409742088716' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<style type="text/css">p { margin-bottom: 0.25cm; line-height: 120%; }</style>


<br />
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Here are a few
thoughts on this never ending &#8220;Crisis&#8221; of the making of
&#8220;Blairites&#8221;, &#8220;Progress members&#8221;, and self called &#8220;Social
Democrats&#8221;.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Firstly, an
overwhelming majority of us voted to return our Party to its
Socialist roots, 60% of us.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
We don&#8217;t for one
minute think that such a change will just be accepted by the voters,
we are not stupid, it will need to be sold to our supporters. There
have been years of anti-Socialist rhetoric, not least from within our
own Party, yet alone the Murdoch empire. Years of so called
&#8220;Pragmatism&#8221; from within have robbed us of our identity. What of
this &#8220;Pragmatic Middle of the Road approach&#8221;??? It's just an
excuse, an excuse for taking the easy option, not rocking the boat,
not being too different to anyone else, not sticking ones head above
the parapet.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
It is needless to
say VERY popular within the Westminster Bubble that is the PLP.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
They don&#8217;t want to
rock the boat or do anything that might jeopardise their lovely cosy
existence as MP's. It even extends to some Councillors.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
They declare that
this approach is the only way, that Corbyn is &#8220;ruining the Party
and must go&#8221;. 
</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
The only &#8220;Party&#8221;
being ruined is theirs. The jolly is over. You have to work for your
keep now, you have to sell our new found principles to the voters.
You WONT have the cosy support of the Murdoch empire as Blair did,
you WONT have the comfort of easy going BBC presenters.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
You will have to
work bloody hard to sell the future to the voters, to convince them
there IS an alternative to the endless austerity the Blairites are so
comfortable with.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Is all this work too
much for you? 
</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Are you more worried
about loosing your cosy positions of power?</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Would you rather
take the easy option and not rock the boat?</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Are you more
concerned that your gravy train might come off the rails than with
the people your supposed to represent?</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Guess what? You are
lazy worthless politicians who have no place in this Party. We have
found our purpose, the one we always had, to fight for ordinary
working people, to take on the powerful and the rich. To better the
lives of the little people, those the Tories despise, trample and
exploit for their gain. THAT is the purpose of Labour.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
We are SUPPOSED to
rock the boat, We are SUPPOSED to be radical, we are SUPPOSED to
stick our heads above the parapet.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
It's called
conviction, conviction in Socialism.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
So, the next time
you hear someone say &#8220;Corbyn must go&#8221;, &#8220;we must take action
against him&#8221;, or any other similar talk of Coups, think very hard.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Why is the person
making these statements doing this?</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
And more to the
point does anyone who constantly demands Corbyn's removal even belong
in the Labour Party?</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Even if they don&#8217;t
like him, they should get on with the job in hand and sell Labour to
the voters.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
If they are not
prepared to do this, the door is open.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Please use it.</div>

 <style type="text/css">p { margin-bottom: 0.25cm; line-height: 120%; }</style>


<br />
<div align="center" style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<b><u>Where
Labour Lost its Way</u></b></div>
<div align="center" style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br />
</div>
<div align="left" style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Under
Blair we became obsessed with &#8220;Winning&#8221;.</div>
<div align="left" style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
A
sports team is ALL about winning, once you have won you have achieved
the ultimate goal, there is no more for you to do.</div>
<div align="left" style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Managers
will adapt the running of sports teams to win, change players, change
tactics.</div>
<div align="left" style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
That
is exactly what &#8220;New Labour&#8221; did. It became ALL about winning,
our politicians became &#8220;Managers&#8221;, and employed many unelected
&#8220;advisor&#8217;s&#8221; to decide Party policy.</div>
<div align="left" style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
But
we are not a sports team, its not about some cup. We are a political
party. We are supposed to change peoples lives for the better, not
adopt certain policies just to make it easier to win.</div>
<div align="left" style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
We
should fight to convince people we are different, that we have real
policies for real people. Even if that makes winning difficult in the
eyes of &#8220;managers&#8221;.</div>
<div align="left" style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
It
is time we ditched &#8220;managers&#8221; who are only interested in the
salary and the glory of winning. We need to become conviction
politicians once more, people who strive to change the  lives of
others, not strive for themselves.  We need selfless politicians,
caring and honest politicians. That is how Labour wins. Labour wins
when the People win, not a few self centred grandees. Political
careers for careers sake, are immoral, selfish and not something that
belongs in Labour. The voters want people interested in what they can
give back, not how many millions they can earn from Politics.</div>
<div align="left" style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
If
Labour is to be a team that wins, that &#8220;Team&#8221; must comprise ALL
our voters. The poor, the old, the young, the sick, the workers and
the jobless.</div>
<div align="left" style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Not
simply a handful of MP's and advisor&#8217;s, concerned only with their
own comfortable futures.</div>
<div style='clear: both;'></div>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Friday, 24 July 2015</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='7426146765397437632' itemprop='blogId'/>
<meta content='1875569020154245165' itemprop='postId'/>
<a name='1875569020154245165'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://bobmcnair.blogspot.co.uk/2015/07/the-descent-of-our-leadership-contest.html'>The Descent of our Leadership contest into the depths of Despair.</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-1875569020154245165' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<style type="text/css">p { margin-bottom: 0.25cm; line-height: 120%; }</style>


<br />
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
I have recently been
enraged by what is going on in our Party.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
I have regretfully
been drawn into the open warfare which is tearing us apart.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Why?</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Because something is
very unjust with the way our Party is functioning, and especially
with the way we choose our leader.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
The nominations for
the leadership, are made solely by the Parliamentary Labour Party.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
This is very very
wrong. It is undemocratic and it is tearing our Party apart.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
I invite everyone to
read the following five questions, you may be familiar with them.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
1/ WHAT POWER HAVE
YOU GOT?<br />
2/ WHERE DID YOU GET IT FROM?<br />
3/ IN WHOSE INTERESTS DO
YOU EXERCISE IT?<br />
4/ TO WHOM ARE YOU ACCOUNTABLE?<br />
5/ HOW CAN WE
GET RID OF YOU? 
</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Now please apply
these questions to our Shadow Cabinet and our Leaders.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
1/</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Firstly they have
great power. They choose who sits in the Cabinet, Shadow or
otherwise. At present they have sole control over who WE are
permitted to vote for as Party Leader. By this virtue alone, they
have SOLE AUTHORITY over determining who is in charge. We the members
do not.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
2/</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
They were given this
power, after at some point being part of a democratic selection
process where a local Party evaluated them and selected them to stand
as a prospective Parliamentary Candidate.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
It is indeed very
rare for a sitting MP to ever be de-selected other than for gross
misconduct.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
So once standing as
an MP they are there for the long term in many instances.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
3/</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Now we come to the
awkward bit. In whose interests exactly ARE they exercising this
power when it comes to choosing our leader? I will suggest it is
certainly not in the interests of root and branch members or the
electorate they represent . Our leadership, as with that of many
Party&#8217;s, has slipped into a form of succession or inheritance. A
bit like an old fashioned Company  Boardroom, or a Club or Society of
some sort. In these institutions it was customary for people on the
board or committee,  to &#8220;work their way up&#8221; and &#8220;serve their
time&#8221;, until they were the next in line for the big chair. In
modern times, even big company boardrooms have moved away from this,
and will often bring in new talent to revive a failing business. Our
MP's, by being in sole charge of who is permitted to stand for
leader, are able to perpetuate a form of agreed succession between
themselves. We the membership, are excluded from absolute choice of
our leader.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
4/</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
The next awkward
bit. They are not really accountable to anyone. They choose their own
leader (by selecting who WE can vote for), they choose their own
positions and roles, and unless they loose a general election (as in
the case of Ed Balls) are there till they choose to retire.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
5/</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Another awkward bit.
As I said earlier, it is very rare indeed to replace a successful MP
with a new candidate. It does happen, but it is obviously a very
undesirable process for a local Branch, why after all replace someone
who is winning elections with a possibly uncertain candidate. People
do have voter loyalty to a sitting MP. Quite rightly if he is doing
his job properly and representing them.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Now we return to
where we are and the leadership contest which is rapidly descending
into a cross between some comical farce, and an episode of a soap
opera where everyone is stabbing everyone else in the back.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
It is clear  that
the leadership selection process is not fit for purpose.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
It is obvious that
there is a chasm between the wishes of the select few in the Shadow
Cabinet, and those of the Membership. The correct path in this case
is not open for debate. We are a Democratic Socialist Party. It is
beyond all doubt that the vote of the Membership is the only correct
way of selecting a leader. It is therefore completely unacceptable,
that the Membership is not able to exercise complete control over the
nominations for who appears on the ballot papers.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
What has happened,
is that we have 3 very similar candidates, chosen by agreement of
those in power, and one candidate who it seems, was begrudgingly put
there to pacify calls for a more varied choice, and to prevent unrest
amongst the membership. Some will of course, have nominated him
because they believe it is the right thing to do for whatever reason.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Now that candidate
is proving to be the choice of the membership, and grass roots, there
is outrage and derision that the &#8220;natural order of succession&#8221;
has been challenged. All of these people would do well to remember
that they once relied on convincing ordinary members like us, that
they were worthy of being put on a ballot paper in a General Election
or Bye Election.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
All of them
respected our choice then, they courted our votes at hustings, and
convinced us to give them a chance.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Where has that
respect gone now? We are &#8220;Loonies&#8221;, we need &#8220;transplants&#8221;,
and we are suddenly treated with derision for exercising our
democratic right as members to choose a leader.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
It is time that this
sanitised selection of who we can vote for is ended. It is plainly
vital that the Membership is allowed to nominate candidates for
leadership. Those in the PLP must have no more say than a member
working in a supermarket. One Member, One Vote.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
We must end this
leadership selection by a Cabal of the powerful. Such a system
belongs to our enemies, it is the Tory way. Had Jeremy Corbyn NOT
received enough nominations, we would have a 3 way leadership contest
between 3 very similar, agreed and sanitised candidates. Non of which
are popular amongst either the grass roots membership, or the Labour
voters whose job it is for the grass roots members to speak to every
day.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
We need a change to
how we do this, our leader must be chosen by ALL our members at every
stage of the process. From nomination (via Primaries of many
candidates if necessary) to a one member one vote final ballot. Only
then do we have credibility, and only this will prevent ANY powerful
faction from taking complete control of our Party&#8217;s leadership
process.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
If we do not offer a
transparently democratic choice to the rule by the 1% offered by The
Tories, we will never be a true and decent alternative to them. An we
will become more like them every day until our Politics differ in
name only, much like in the USA.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
One thing is
certain.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Absolute Power
Corrupts Absolutely</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
The Labour Party
must make this impossible within our ranks.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
All our leaders must
be truly accountable on a regular basis. 
</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
And removable by the
Membership. 
</div>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Sunday, 21 June 2015</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='7426146765397437632' itemprop='blogId'/>
<meta content='7413499252438976437' itemprop='postId'/>
<a name='7413499252438976437'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://bobmcnair.blogspot.co.uk/2015/06/our-party-stands-at-doors-of-last.html'>Our Party stands at the doors of "The Last Chance Saloon".</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-7413499252438976437' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<style type="text/css">p { margin-bottom: 0.25cm; line-height: 120%; }</style>


<br />
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
It has been some
time since my last post, and much has changed.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Our Country is now
run by a Tory Government which is hell bent on further rapid
implementation of  its prime dogma, Austerity.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
This Austerity is
based on a lie (in fact a stack of them), and it is sadly undeniably
true, that the majority of our Parliamentary leadership have bought
into this lie.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Some with
misgivings, some with as much enthusiasm as the Tories themselves.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
During my
campaigning, I spoke to many voters, on the street and on doorsteps.
Many of them were apathetic about voting at all, many were drawn to
extremist Party&#8217;s. Young people were most adamant about one thing
&#8220;your all the same&#8221;, I heard that so many times, as I know
canvassers up and down the Country did. Not just from young first
time voters, but pensioners as well.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Where did we go
wrong? Simple if you ask me. We WERE too similar. People had had 5
years of hurt, with the certainty of much more on the way. They did
not want the prospect we offered of guess what?? More of the same,
from a Party they used to look to as an alternative to the Tories.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Yes we offered some
good policies, and I fully supported Ed Miliband and still like the
guy. But as for policies that stood out and set us apart? nowhere
near enough of them, nor radical enough.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Worse still I was
ashamed to hear people in our own leadership announcing that as a
Party &#8220;we did not seek to represent those on benefits&#8221;, that we
&#8220;could not condone scrounging&#8221;, that there must be &#8220;change to
the welfare system to make work pay&#8221;, that &#8220;hard working people&#8221;
(I HATE that bloody phrase), were &#8220;fed up of spongers and
scroungers&#8221;. This was not quotes from the Express or Mail, it was
from supposed LABOUR Politicians. A Month or so later they were
wringing their hands stabbing Ed in the back, and wondering why we
lost, and of all things saying it was because we were &#8220;too far
Left&#8221;. Farcical. They are only worried about their own careers.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Can I beg to suggest
to them, they THEY were to blame? To blame because we were not
different enough. We did not offer any sort of structured ALTERNATIVE
to Austerity, just more Austerity with a slightly different bias,
&#8220;Tory-Lite&#8221; if you will.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Why has this
happened? Well there is an explanation of sorts in my opinion, here
it is.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Over recent years,
the Tories, partly dragged by their UKIP Cousins, have moved very
firmly to the Right in just about every way. Today&#8217;s Tory Party is
a far more &#8220;Conservative&#8221; than ever, propped up and directed by
the wishes of big business and lobby groups.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
There is within the
Labour Party, a faction which has always stated erroneously in my
belief,  that we cant win Elections unless we appeal to &#8220;The Centre
Ground&#8221;. Well, this is how that works, and why we are in this mess
now.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Our opposition moves
firmly to the Right, the &#8220;Centre Ground&#8221; by definition follows
them. So, we as a Party, in order to fill this Centre Ground, adopt
policies and strategies which are themselves ever further to the
Right. This benefits no-one but the Tories and their supporters, who
can never really loose, as our policies are very similar in essence
to theirs. By signing up to Austerity we do their job for them, and
constrain our own ambitions in an ideological straight-jacket of
their making.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
How do we defeat and
counter this and offer policies that make us stand out as a viable
alternative because we are different? We must reject the Tory Made
Austerity. It benefits no-one but the wealthy. To do this we need a
radical leadership.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
We have a Candidate
for leader who can do this. His name is Jeremy Corbyn, he is standing
on an Anti-Austerity platform.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
He is a radical, he
is starkly different. He is proud to be a Socialist, and he is a
champion of the poor, the unemployed, those on benefits, those in
jobs, the disabled , and of the Working Class in general. He is
feared by the Tories and their supporters.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
He is also feared by
those in our own Party. The &#8220;Oxbridge Elite&#8221; cannot relate to
him, nor can they relate to Socialism in general. There is good
reason for this.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
I have stated before
that many of our current leadership think and act the same as the
Tories for good reason. Most were educated in the same Universities,
by the same lecturers alongside the Tory Elite. Is it any wonder they
think the same way, but with slightly different priorities?</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
In the early days of
our Party, our leaders came from the same World as our voters, many
were Trade Union officials, men who had worked in factories alongside
the Voters, men passionate about Socialism who dedicated their lives
to fight for change. They were not university boys, they were not
Economics, Law and Sociology graduates, yet they gave us the NHS and
the employment rights, human rights and social structure, all things
we take for granted, and that the Tories are so desperate to
dismantle.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
We were different,
we were radical and we offered true change, we won the trust of the
voters and we innovated and educated and effected true change.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
We have stagnated
more and more the further to the Right we have become. The more our
leadership has become complacent to Austerity, even complicit in
Austerity. Our leadership hopefuls have bought to far into the Tory
dream, and cannot see the way forward. Many are either terrified to
admit to being Socialists, or worse declare they are NOT Socialists.
Many I am sure are not.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
To further Socialism
is the prime reason The Labour Party exists, your membership card
used to say this (Until Blair had it removed).</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Without this
commitment to Socialism, this commitment to change, we are nothing.
We are never going to inspire.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Disenchanted voters
are voting for radical alternatives, The Greens, and worse for UKIP,
a far Right racist Party. And YES you are racist, most of your
supporters are, I spoke to enough of them whilst canvassing and heard
enough &#8220;I'm not a racist but&#8221; statements to last a lifetime.
Working man has been set against working man on the basis of
ethnicity in the cruellest con trick ever.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Divide and Rule they
call it. The Tories used it to build The Empire, now they are using
it to control us here in Britain.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
We are as an
effective Opposition Party, that can offer any sort of an alternative
which will galvanise our core voters, staring into the doors of &#8220;The
Last Chance Saloon&#8221;. 
</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
We can go in one at
a time, quietly and meekly, apologising for our once proud beliefs,
and be gunned down by the Tory Big Boys.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
Or we can go in as
one with the big guns of our once proud Socialist principles blasting
Equality and Democracy for all, and an end to the unjust Austerity. 
</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
We must win this
fight, or we must fail with our boots on and our flags flying, not go
meekly into an endless Tory future betrayed by our own.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
I am ready to kick
the shutter doors open carrying the red flag of freedom, who will
march into the last Chance Saloon with me???</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
I know Jeremy Corbyn
will.</div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
<br /></div>
<div style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0cm;">
More to follow.</div>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Sunday, 10 May 2015</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://bobmcnair.blogspot.co.uk/2015/05/blair-again.html'>Blair Again</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-3441041008179342833' itemprop='description articleBody'>
We are days away from one of our biggest defeats ever, and Blair is already saying that we lost as we were too far to the left and need to be "in the centre ground".<br />
This does not fit with my view of things. The SNP and the Greens both adopted an Anti-Austerity stance, and grew their vote. The SNP destroyed us in Scotland and contributed largely to our collapse. Whilst the Greens don&#8217;t have huge numbers of MP's, they drew an increased vote and undoubtedly hurt us by stealing votes from youngsters we needed to vote for us in order to win.<br />
Locally here in Christchurch they hurt us in the local elections, pushing us into third place behind UKIP in a ward we should have won.<br />
<br />
Now Blair is telling us we need to be more lilly livered than we already are.<br />
He is wrong.<br />
<br />
For many we were simply not different enough to The Tories, not radical enough.<br />
Our version of "Austerity-Lite" did not appeal. People have had enough of bearing the brunt whilst they saw Millionaires get tax cuts (assuming they paid <b>ANY</b> in the first place). Whilst they were hit with cuts, pain and a never ending assault by the Coalition. WE were just not seen to have offered a clear enough alternative, allthough we in reality, were very different.<br />
<br />
So Mr Blair, if you take control of this Party again, with your Neo-Liberal pink Tory claptrap, I will resign from it.<br />
If you do so, I sincerely hope that the Unions pull all Labours funding and start a new party.<br />
<br />
One with decent Socialist principles, one that has something like the "Clause 4" that you removed from Labour. That party SHOULD be Labour, there are many like me who will resist any attempt by the Blairites to take control of our Party, with all our might and to the last breath.<br />
<br />
But rest assured, if you do, many like me will resign, and we will form a new Party. We don&#8217;t want to believe me, we all fully believe that it is Labours job to win this fight against the Tories. But if you turn our proud Socialist Party into some sort of pink Neo-Liberal apology for the Party we should be, we WILL go.<br />
<br />
I hope that this nightmare from our past disappears where he belongs, in the dustbin of history.<br />
<br />
Go Away Mr Blair! and take your Progress chums with you.<br />
Go and join the Lib-Dems, they need some new lilly livered supporters.

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Saturday, 9 May 2015</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='7426146765397437632' itemprop='blogId'/>
<meta content='5085524638348879905' itemprop='postId'/>
<a name='5085524638348879905'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://bobmcnair.blogspot.co.uk/2015/05/post-7th-of-may.html'>Post 7th of May</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-5085524638348879905' itemprop='description articleBody'>
We sit currently, nursing ourselves from one of the biggest and unexpected defeats ever.<br />
Th Nation sits under the rule of one of the most Right Wing, cruel and oppressive Tory regimes in modern history. One that would be unimaginable in its severity even to Margaret Thatcher.<br />
I have just posted these words on our facebook page, they are my own personal view.<br />
<br />
Here we go, the BBC are now saying (quoting Alan Johnson) that "its a 10 year job for Labour to rebuild".<br />We DONT HAVE 10 ******* YEARS. The poor, the disabled, the sick, the old, any one who is just a bit different cant wait that long.<br />The Country will be nearly destroyed after 5, after 10 don&#8217;t even bother getting out of bed to challenge it, just march straight to the nearest poor house if your not too bloody sick or old.<br />If we don&#8217;t get our act together now, and put up a valid and radical alternative, one that is SO different it cant be mistaken, we are wasting our time.<br />Now if ever, is the time to bring back "Clause 4" of our once proud constitution.<br /><br />Clause 4 (as was)<br />"To secure for the workers by hand or by brain the full fruits of their industry and the most equitable distribution thereof that may be possible upon the basis of the common ownership of the means of production, distribution and exchange, and the best obtainable system of popular administration and control of each industry of service." <br /><br />We ditched this in 1995, under the leadership of one Tony (Tory Bastard) Blair. A socialist he was not.<br /><br />Time to bring it back?<br />If we cant be radical, cant be different, and cant offer hope to the weak.<br />What the hell is the bloody point???<br /><br />If you are obviously radical, obviously different, it is damn difficult for your opposition to deceive people as to your intentions. Your true motives can be seen and understood. It is time for us to have true conviction once more. We cant go on being " Tory-Lite" any more. We are not "Neo-Liberals".<br />If we are not going to be proper decent Socialists, with a democratically elected through the ballot box revolutionary agenda. <br /><br />Why do we exist??????<br />
<br />
The statement I am making is this. The Labour Party was founded as a Revolutionary Socialist Party, who believed in change through the democratic process.<br />
It was via this belief, that our greatest achievements, such as the NHS, and the Welfare State were founded. These beliefs also inspired our principles of equality and justice, our hatred of prejudice of all forms, and were why we campaigned and achieved decent Human Rights legislation.<br />
<br />
All of this is in disarray and at risk of loss in the next 5 Years.<br />
I truly believe we need to re-visit Clause 4, and incorporate a modern re-written version into our constitution.<br />
<br />
The Daily Mail and Murdochs finest will squeal in horror. Good, that means we have it right.<br />
The time for appeasement is over, the time for being moderate is long gone, our opponents are certainly not moderate, and nor can we be if we hope to offer an alternative.<br />
No More Mr Nice Guy.<br />
It is time we are plain and clear what we stand for and what we offer the voters.<br />
If we dont, as I said.<br />
Why do we exist??? Simply to offer jobs to politicians who don&#8217;t want to be Tories but are too scared to be real Socialists?<br />
There is a party for people like that.<br />
Its called The Liberals.

<dd class='profile-textblock'>I am Endorsing and voting for Jeremy Corbyn as our new leader.

It is my mission to help prepare those generations to take up the fight against the Ignorance, Fear, Greed, and Hatred which have returned this Tory Parliament. I cannot win this fight for our Young People, But I will try and help them win that fight themselves.
