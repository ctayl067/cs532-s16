The "Prix des Innovateurs"

   Students, young graduates and young entrepreneurs now have until 29
   February 2016 to enter the “Prix des Innovateurs” competition
   Launched in December 2015, in spring 2016 the Innovators Award will
   reward project owners in the fields of Big Data and Energy Storage.
   A judging panel of eight personalities will select the best projects
   according to various criteria: the inventiveness of the project,
   maturity, feasibility, potential extensions and the quality of
   presentation.
   Students, graduates and young entrepreneurs now have until 29 February
   2016 to submit their entries on the “Prix des Innovateurs” website:
   [26]http://prixdesinnovateurs.arcelormittal.com/deposer-votre-candidatu
   re/
   Qualifying projects can be entered at two different stages of
   development: Ideas to be developed, which will subsequently require
   incubation, design and laboratory research or advanced design, the
   following stages being support, production engineering, prototyping,
   market testing and funding.
   In each of the two categories, the winners will receive a grant of
   50,000 euros for the first, 10,000 euros for the second and 2,500 euros
   for the third in order to help them to bring their project to fruition.
   They will also be able to benefit from the support of ArcelorMittal
   experts.
   The judging panel comprises personalities from the business world,
   local authorities, higher education, the media, and representatives of
   ArcelorMittal:
   Hervé Bourrier
     __________________________________________________________________

   Hervé Bourrier is a graduate of the Bordeaux Ecole Nationale Supérieure
   de Chimie et de Physique and also has an MBA from Cedep/Insead. He is
   now CEO of ArcelorMittal France, President of the UIR (Union of
   Recycling Industries) and Vice-President of EPE (Enterprises for the
   Environment).
   Carl de Maré
     __________________________________________________________________

   A civil engineer specialising in electromechanical engineering, Carl de
   Maré joined the Group in 1988. He is now Vice President of
   ArcelorMittal, Head of innovation and technological development.
   Gilles Le Borgne
     __________________________________________________________________

   A graduate of the Ecole Nationale Supérieure des Céramiques
   Industrielles, Gilles Le Borgne is now Director of Research and
   Development at PSA Peugeot Citroën group. He is also jointly
   responsible for the 2l/100 project within the New Industrial France
   programme, and Chairman of the Automotive Technical Committee within
   the Automotive Industry Platform.
   François Mudry
     __________________________________________________________________

   A graduate of the Ecole Polytechnique and author of a thesis on
   "Modelling fracture in nuclear pressure vessel steels" François Mudry
   has notably been the scientific adviser in respect of ArcelorMittal R&D
   projects. He is currently President of the Lorraine Technological
   Research Institute and Adviser to the High Commissioner of the French
   Atomic Energy Commission.
   Eric Niedziela
     __________________________________________________________________

   An engineering graduate from ENSAM Aix en Provence, Eric Niedziela
   began his career at the Fos plant. He joined Business Division North in
   2009 as Chief Technology Officer, and is now CEO of ArcelorMittal
   Atlantique & Lorraine.
   Danièle Quantin
     __________________________________________________________________

   An engineering graduate from Ecole Centrale Paris, Danièle Quantin has
   been a member of ArcelorMittal’s research and development steering
   committee since 2007. She is now Global R&D Director of Human Resources
   and coordination of research centres in France and Spain.
   Lucie Viry
     __________________________________________________________________

   With a PhD in the physical chemistry of condensed matter and functional
   polymers, Lucie Viry was particularly interested in the study and
   development of innovative solutions applied to robotics and the medical
   sector. She is now responsible for R&D in respect of materials
   solutions for a new additive manufacturing technology (3D printing) for
   Pollen AM.
   Patrick Weiten
     __________________________________________________________________

   A municipal engineering graduate, Patrick Weiten was recently elected
   to be the first Vice-President of the Acal (Alsace Champagne-Ardenne
   Lorraine) Regional Council. He was President of the Moselle Department
   Council from 2011 to 2015, Mayor of Yutz from 1995 to 2011, and
   President of the Portes de France Thionville integrated local authority
   from 2008 to 2015.
