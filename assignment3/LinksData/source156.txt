<h2 class='date-header'><span>Friday, August 14, 2015</span></h2>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://fossilfriendly.blogspot.com/2015/08/the-west-coast-is-burning-oh-not-coast.html'>The West Coast is On Fire and I'm scared</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-3981570703395597255' itemprop='description articleBody'>
The West Coast is Burning<br />
<br />
Oh, not the coast part of the West Coast. At least, not yet. But The West Coast, ie everything from Alaska through BC and on down the west coast states and provinces of North America, through Washington, Oregon, California, all the way to Baja Mexico. The West Coast is on fire.<br />
<br />
As far as I can tell no one east of the West Coast even cares.<br />
<br />
Wildfires happen, but not like this. People in Baker City, Oregon are taking refuge at the elementary school. The wettest place in North America that isn't under a lake, the Olympic Rainforest in Washington, is burning. It's one of the few temperate rainforests in the world, and it's on fire.<br />
<br />
I can smell the woodsmoke in my bedroom in Oregon. The fires the smoke is coming from are hundreds of miles away.<br />
<br />
That's how bad it is.<br />
<br />
The West Coast is on fire, and I'm fucking terrified. Does the rest of the US even care?<br />
<br />

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Saturday, January 10, 2015</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='2446302058815899740' itemprop='blogId'/>
<meta content='5504190607853199131' itemprop='postId'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://fossilfriendly.blogspot.com/2015/01/red-cross.html'>Red Cross</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-5504190607853199131' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
Free write 5/18/2014</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
When all was said and done there wasn't
much to say. I stared at my partner's widow, and she stared back at
me, pale and blood-splattered, her normally dark skin ashen gray. &#8220;I
hate you,&#8221; she said, and she said it so simply as if it was nothing
more than a statement of fact. My lips and fingertips tingled and I
could feel myself growing cold as the blood flowed out through the IV
and onto the floor. I was going to bleed to death, right here in this
death chamber than until a half an hour ago was a Red Cross
blood-mobile.  She stared at me, her face perfectly expressionless
even as she raised end of the IV to her mouth and sucked, like a
child sucking cherry juice through a straw. It wasn't her fault, I
knew. It was mine.</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
And her husband's. My partner's.</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
We really didn't mean of any of this to
happen, but intentions and the road to hell and all that.</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
I licked my lips, feeling incredibly
thirsty, but there wasn't a chance in hell I was getting cookies and
juice now. &#8220;What did you do with his body?&#8221; I asked. He was dead,
I knew that for a fact, no one but no one survives having their head
hacked off and placed on a pike. But I had no idea where the hell the
rest of him was. I suppose, given the current circumstances, it
shouldn't really matter to me, but it did. It really did. &#8220;Did you
have him cremated?&#8221;</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
She pinched the IV shut, folding it in
half and squeezing tight. The flow of my blood stopped momentarily
along with my death. A temporary reprieve.</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
&#8220;I know you and Rich thought I was
stupid,&#8221; she said to me, her calm hatred lashing me with a hundred
million whips. &#8220;But I'm not. His blood was contaminated, and he
contaminated me. Of course I had him cremated, I wasn't going to let
him be chopped to pieces in your lab.&#8221;</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
My lab. Our lab. She put the IV back to
her lips, unfolding it and letting the blood flow once more. I knew
was she was doing of course, and despite what she may have felt me
and Rich never thought she was stupid.</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<i>You infected me, you pieces of shit,</i>
she'd spat at us when we realized what had happened to Rich, and
Mary. And it had been true. But in our defense we hadn't meant to, we
hadn't meant for the virus to go wild, or for it to have the effects
that it did. But again, intentions and the hell and roads and so
forth. Rich had been quivering in the final stages of the disease as
he pleaded with his wife, he hadn't meant any of this.</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
I strained briefly
against the straps. The Red Cross doesn't strap anyone down, but that
hadn't stopped the phlebotomists any this time around. They'd looked
at me with dead eyes the moment I stepped out of the little
questionnaire room  and grabbed me, unnaturally strong for a bunch of
middle aged health care professionals. Then Mary stepped out and
stared at me, and I knew there was no going back.</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
&#8220;Rich had the
virus,&#8221; she said calmly. Unlike the movies her teeth looked normal.
Her canines had not lengthened, her incisors had not sharpened. The
virus did not attack the teeth one bit, even as it drove the
metabolism insane. &#8220;He gave it to me. But you don't, and I wondered
why. Why did I get it, and you didn't? You fucked him as much as I
did. You should have gotten it.&#8221;</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
Again, truth. She
wasn't stupid.</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
She took the IV out
of her mouth again. The blood in her mouth turned her teeth slick and
red. It flooded out of the tube for a moment, then she pinched it off
again. I felt cold and short of breath, and I wondered how much I had
bleed already. The other donors had already left, the phlebotomists
with their dead eyes had taken their donations as if everything was
perfectly normal and not a single one of the other donors had been
hurt or killed. I was relieved in a way, Mary's anger had been
directed solely at me. The virus had not driven her insane with
bloodlust, like we thought it would. Perhaps everything would be okay
in this new world Rich and I had created.</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
Even if we didn't
get to see it.</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
&#8220;So I wondered,
was the vampire virus the only one you created? And the answer was of
course not. You were playing God,&#8221; and here she snarled. &#8220;God!
And I know you scientists, you worked with a variety of strains, not
merely one. You're infected too. Just, you know, with something
else.&#8221; She laugh humorlessly. &#8220;And you're such a cowardly shit
that you'd rather I bleed you to death than save yourself.&#8221;</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
Muscles knotted
under my skin. It was true. It was equally true that I was letting
her kill me. Mary had proved that the v-virus was safe in the modern
world, relatively speaking. She would only kill those who had wronged
her, and only at the level of betrayal that I and Rich had cast upon
her.</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
Too late, my bones
turned to dust and cracked, cracked, cracked  beneath my skin, my
muscles rippled. I licked my lips again.</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
&#8220;You better kill
me quick,&#8221; I said, my voice dim and distant in my own ears. My
muscles were tearing inside me, my skin was turning purple with the
bruising. I didn't know I had that much blood still left in me.</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
She laughed and
pressed a bit of gauze against the needle in my arm, pulling my arm
up above my head. She pulled the needle out with a jerk and pressed
the gauze tight, and smiled at me. Chilling.</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
&#8220;No,&#8221; Mary
said. &#8220;I want you to become your own monster. You've done this to
me, and you killed Rich, so I get to watch.&#8221;</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
My muscles clenched
again in one spastic cramp, rending and tearing myself from the
inside out. The w-virus was tearing me apart, one cell at a time.</div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<br />
<div style="margin-bottom: 0in;">
And Mary got to
watch.</div>
