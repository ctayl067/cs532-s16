<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://eeebgum.blogspot.co.uk/2016/02/slow-cooked-pineapple-pork.html'>Slow cooked pineapple pork</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-87996356936398750' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<h3>
Ingredients</h3>
<div>
400g lean pork, cubed</div>
<div>
1 red onion, peeled and finely chopped</div>
<div>
3 cloves garled, peeled and finely chopped</div>
<div>
2" root ginger, peeled and finely chopped</div>
<div>
1 red pepper, de-seeded and cut into strips</div>
<div>
2 tbsp tomato ketchup</div>
<div>
227g can of pinapple chunks in juice</div>
<div>
2 tbsp soy sauce ( I use gluten free tamari)<br />1 tbsp apple cider vinegar</div>
<div>
2 tbsp boullion powder ( I use Marigold gluten free)<br />3 tsp cornflour mixed with enough water to make a thin paste<br />Water to cover</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<h3>
Method</h3>
<div>
Place all the ingredients in a slow cooker, with enough water to cover the other ingredients. Stir. Cook on low for approx 5 hours. Remove the lid and turn up to high and cook for a further hour.<br /><br />Serve with plain boiled rice.<br /><br />Serves 4.<br /><br /><a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-qwGmHllvwp0/VsDT6TwtjDI/AAAAAAAAB2U/BICXxpXdzPI/s1600/pinapple%2Bpork%2B.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em; text-align: center;"><img border="0" height="174" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-qwGmHllvwp0/VsDT6TwtjDI/AAAAAAAAB2U/BICXxpXdzPI/s320/pinapple%2Bpork%2B.JPG" width="320" /></a></div>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://eeebgum.blogspot.co.uk/2016/02/recipe-coconut-meringue-cookies-gluten.html'>Recipe - Coconut meringue cookies - gluten and dairy free</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-4561765407522381665' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<h3>
Ingredients</h3>
<div>
<u>Base:</u></div>
<div>
85 g (3 oz) &nbsp;coconut oil</div>
<div>
115 g (4 oz) coconut palm sugar (or caster sugar)<br />
3 medium egg yolks</div>
<div>
2 tbsp coconut milk</div>
<div>
Few drops vanilla extract</div>
<div>
140 g (5 oz) brown rice flour</div>
<div>
30 g (1 oz) tapioca flour</div>
<div>
11/2 tsp gluten free baking powder</div>
<div>
<u>Topping:</u></div>
<div>
3 medium egg whites</div>
<div>
60 g (2 oz) dessicated coconut</div>
<div>
115 g (4 oz) raw cane sugar or caster sugar</div>
<div>
12 glace cherries, halved</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
Preheat the oven to 160 C /140 fan/ gas 3. Grease and line a Swiss roll tin.<br />
Mix the coconut oil and coconut sugar together. If the oil is stiff warm it a little first - it is easiest if it is nearly liquid. Beat in the egg yolks, milk, and vanilla. Fold in the flours and baking powder. Spread the mixture over the base of the tin.</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
Beat the egg whites until stiff. &nbsp;Fold in the coconut and sugar. Spread onto the base using a spatula.</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-aUN6ruHeYOw/U8Jf0bE3eeI/AAAAAAAABFg/hbyKe8_KEdQ/s1600/001.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="240" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-aUN6ruHeYOw/U8Jf0bE3eeI/AAAAAAAABFg/hbyKe8_KEdQ/s1600/001.JPG" width="320" /></a></div>
<div>
<br />
<br /></div>
<div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
Decorate with the cherry halves. Bake for about 30 minutes until light golden on top. Allow to cool before cutting into pieces and removing from tin. Makes 24</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://eeebgum.blogspot.co.uk/2016/01/sweet-and-spicy-lamb-stew.html'>Sweet and spicy lamb stew</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-6959133705353988577' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<h3>
Ingredients</h3>
<div>
1 red onion, peeled and finely chopped</div>
<div>
3 cloves garlic, peeled and finely chopped</div>
<div>
500g diced lamb<br />120g sultanas</div>
<div>
2 tsp ground cinammon</div>
<div>
2 tsp ground coriander</div>
<div>
1 tsp mild chilli powder</div>
<div>
1 tsp ground ginger</div>
<div>
2 tbsp lemon juice</div>
<div>
5oo ml lamb stock (or use chicken stock if you don't have lamb stock)</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<h3>
Method</h3>
Fry the onion, garlic and lamb until the meat is browned and the onion soft.<br />Add the remaining ingredients and stir well.<br />Simmer for 3 hours, stirring occasionally and adding a little water if the sauce is getting too 'dry'.<br />Serve with rice or pitta breads.<br /><br /><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
Serves 4.<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-nJdeY3PxYs0/VpJ4R6ccc5I/AAAAAAAAB0A/DwLfZI2V-9Q/s1600/DSCF2138.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="240" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-nJdeY3PxYs0/VpJ4R6ccc5I/AAAAAAAAB0A/DwLfZI2V-9Q/s320/DSCF2138.JPG" width="320" /></a></div>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Friday, January 1, 2016</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Na8Lxr7W0Gg/U0W8WQbyy2I/AAAAAAAAAms/yiQDDElQ_EU/s1600/mm+003.JPG' itemprop='image_url'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://eeebgum.blogspot.co.uk/2016/01/masterclass-icing-and-biscuit-set.html'>Masterclass Icing and biscuit set review and gluten/dairy free biscuit recipe</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-5265049255674607509' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
My daughter very kindly bought me a new kitchen gizmo for my birthday. A 'Masterclass icing and biscuit set'.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Na8Lxr7W0Gg/U0W8WQbyy2I/AAAAAAAAAms/yiQDDElQ_EU/s1600/mm+003.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="240" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Na8Lxr7W0Gg/U0W8WQbyy2I/AAAAAAAAAms/yiQDDElQ_EU/s1600/mm+003.JPG" width="320" /></a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="text-align: start;">It comes in a smart plastic box and looks jolly professional. I decided to try out making biscuits first. There's a good selection of piping nozzles and lots of biscuit discs. &nbsp;It took me a couple of attempts to get a gluten and dairy free recipe that was the right consistency to pipe. If the mixture is too runny it doesn't hold the shape and if it is too dry it just falls apart.&nbsp;</span></div>
<br style="text-align: start;" />
<span style="text-align: start;">After making the dough (recipe at the end of this article) you need to fill up the tube by pressing it in with your fingers.</span><br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="text-align: start;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-F7bwnB3we5A/U0W9mPESwxI/AAAAAAAAAm8/vsbT92cBImc/s1600/mm+007.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="240" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-F7bwnB3we5A/U0W9mPESwxI/AAAAAAAAAm8/vsbT92cBImc/s1600/mm+007.JPG" width="320" /></a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
Then screw on the end piece with your choice of biscuit disc.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-5S6IIBG4R-4/U0W9UCyUgCI/AAAAAAAAAm0/aWVTwxvW7EY/s1600/mm+008.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="240" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-5S6IIBG4R-4/U0W9UCyUgCI/AAAAAAAAAm0/aWVTwxvW7EY/s1600/mm+008.JPG" width="320" /></a></div>
<br />
After this it's simply a matter of squeezing the trigger until it clicks, and out pops a biscuit. I find they form best if applied to a sheet of baking paper that has not been greased.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-PBTH18KdwZk/U0W-NK9fZMI/AAAAAAAAAnE/-WidKPbuqas/s1600/mm+009.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="240" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-PBTH18KdwZk/U0W-NK9fZMI/AAAAAAAAAnE/-WidKPbuqas/s1600/mm+009.JPG" width="320" /></a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<br />
And here is my recipe:<br />
<br />
1/2 cup dairy-free margarine (I use Vitalite)<br />
1/4 cup coconut oil<br />
1/2 cup raw cane sugar<br />
1 tbsp coconut milk<br />
1/2 tsp almond essence<br />
1/4 tsp vanilla essence<br />
1 cup brown rice flour<br />
1/2 cup tapioca flour<br />
1/2 cup corn flour<br />
1/2 tsp baking powder<br />
<br />
Cream sugar with fats. Stir in the rest and form together with your hands. Spoon into the tube. Pipe. Bake in a preheated oven at 180 degrees for 12 - 15 minutes.<br />
<br />
I need to go off and practice icing next. :)<br />
<br />
Tip - for cleaning the tube I suggest one of these:<br />
